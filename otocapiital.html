Internship at OTO Capital

OVERVIEW


OTO Capital is a financial-based company which leverages you to own a car faster than ever in a country like India. I worked as a UX Developer for 3 months. I demonstrated my skills by creating wireframes, visual designs and prototyped their core offering applications.

By giving me an opportunity to lead the design team and work closely with developer to suggest them changes, OTO Capital has not only saw the potential in me but also allowed me to contribute my ideas to enhance the product features.

Design Process

Every UX Developer has a different approach to solving the problem. Depending upon the industry, project, and end-users the design process changes. During my work at OTO Capital, I followed the following approach.


Project Learnings

I believe in constant learning. While commuting from my home to office and back, I used to talk with various people and understand their aspect of the product which I was designing for. The communication with local people helped me change the design and opened many roads to think about how things can be changed. My Team was very supportive and always stood by my side whenever I was having trouble in design or understanding the flow. Mr.Sumit CEO of OTO capital gave me a mantra to win. "Designers can drive business decisions; you need to find opportunities and keep pushing new ideas".




Takeaways

Embrace Ambiguity

Move forward without knowing all the answers. Keep clarity of the functionality and process. But never stop your approach. Keep thinking and pushing new ideas.

Ask Right Questions

Even though many questions will be unanswered, or few will be difficult to understand but keep asking questions then wrong designs.

Constant Feedback

Get feedback often and early in the design process. Don't be afraid to show incomplete or unfinished work to your leaders and team members. Understand others' point of view.

Prioritize Process

Keep your designs simple. Don't overload users with too much information. Define your critical journeys early in the process and question every step or screen you create.
