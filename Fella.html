Fella Mobile Application

OVERVIEW


Fella is a mobile application developed for the International student, serving them as an assistant for various challenges that they might face while traveling to a foreign country to pursue their education. It is an application which connects students together from a different community. It also allows you to connect to your senior, update about new events, festivals and local holidays.

The initial idea was to make an application where students can connect together for getting basic information about the new location, rules, and regulation of that city, find roommates for themselves and find out local places such as banks, supermarket, and telecom services.

The Challenge


Identify the major challenges faced by new International students at SUNY Oswego University and design a solution which could assist them, give them information wherever they might face a problem, thus making the transition easier and convenient for the student.



I was inspired by my own classmates where, every day for months, we suffered while we arrived and settling ourselves. User interview was necessity to understand what other people feel when they are coming to other countries and what are the major challenges for them.

Research and Outcome of User Servy


Identifying the user needs

In our research, we interviewed several students, conducted online survey and observations to examine the life of the international students and identifying their needs. We questioned them about the various factors which influence their academic, social and cultural transitions to the U.S. We wanted information about following questions:

  • What are the major challenges faced by international students?
  • Do students feel anxiety or out f context while talking to strangers?
  • How do they find help or information about everyday needs?
  • Do they feel homesick, stressed or lonely? If yes, how do they cope up with that?

Interviews:

We interviewed 20 international students from different countries studying various fields at SUNY Campus. Most of the students said that they faced problems in finding accommodation, preferred food, information about cheapest carrier companies, how to apply for driving license etc. During the interview, they constantly gave us feedback on which features we should come up with first. The survey result and feedback from the users helped us get exact count and prioritize the work.

Observation:

Observation were conducted in different environment to identify problems which students face every day but don't usually notice or talk about it. The findings showed that the students were not familiar with the communication norms and faced difficulty understanding local slang and various American references.

Qualitative Interviews

User research was quintessential. The complex areas are to understand the students from the diverse background and what benefit they will have from the application. In order to achieve that, I gathered as much as I could from international students to understand the challenges they face and how they see the app making a difference in optimizing pain areas.

Insights

After brainstorming and with the help of affinity mapping, we finally decided on a suitable solution for our problem space and what features or functionality will it possess.

Conceptualization

I started creating the information architecture and low-fi concepts for primary use cases. After having a go-ahead from the other team members in the team and developers on the mockups, we began to conduct usability tests with the low-fidelity mockups. Once we had confidence in the design, we began digitalizing designs.

Visual Designs

The most difficult task in design lifecycle is to determine on which device you will design for. Since Fella is a mobile based application, we had to design it for only small screens. The user shift from one device to other & it is difficult to keep the user experience same. The features & functionality changed based on the OS version and screen size. We made the application responsive from design perspective.


User Onboaridng Flow


We created simple and smooth design as it was going to be used by SUNY Oswego students, primarily to create group, chat among different students for finding roommates and getting basic housing things done.

Usability Testing

I tested the project at every stage of the design. It gave us good results and also chance to make changes at very beginning of the design phase. We could engage the user and hence the final the application were used the most after final designs.



Lo-fi Prototypes

Lo-fi designs were tested with the team members and few friends of mine weekly to get feedback on the functionality, content, and interactivity of the product.

Unmoderated User testing

A few dummy groups were created to use the app. All participants were using the app to carry out hypothetical tasks.

Project Learnings

I learned many things while working on my first project in states. Working in a team was never a problem, but working with people coming from different background and country was a new experience. There were many breakdowns which were managed very well. Following are few crusial learnings as UX Developer which were life lessons:


1. Simplicity is strength

As a designer, we are often lured by attractive, trendy and out of the box designs. But, We must always remember the â€˜whyâ€™. The primary goal is to understand the user, their problems and then come up with a design that solves it.


2. Prioritize

Create a strategic plan to launch an MVP. This helps deal with out-of-scope requests that could potentially derail the project and helps deliver a quality product in time.


3. Seek out feedback early and continually

The trouble with most of us is that we would rather be ruined by praise than saved by criticism. Keeping the stakeholders/users in loop and testing solutions in whatever form (paper, low-fi or hi-fi) as early as possible saves ample amount of time and re-work.
