Safer - Smart Assistant

OVERVIEW


SAFER - Smart Assistant for ER is a beacon-enabled multi-platform service for ER nurses. It streamlines the charting and medicine retrieval process, and makes patient tracking and peer communication more efficient, ensuring effective work and stress management of ER nurses and more time for actual patient care.

The primary goal was to perform design research, service design, and prototyping to understand the primary pain points of ER nurses in a 12-hour shift with a focus on their need for balancing patient care and administration requirements with self-care.

The Challenge


Today, as patient acuities are rising, while the support resources become more constrained, nurses working in Emergency Rooms today have become exposed to a higher risk of burnout. The rising demands from both the patients and the hospital are leading to a deterioration in their overall emotional and mental health and also resulting in more medical errors at the workplace.



After taking permission from the local hospital in Oswego City, We took interviews of nurses and conducted basic survey to analyse the reason behind it. We found few intresting facts.

Research and Qualitative Interviews

After our secondary research, we conducted qualitative interviews to study the underlying reasons for the low emotional health and stress management of ER nurses. Recruiting ER nurses was also one of the biggest challenges we faced in our process.

Safer Process Flow and Administration

Key Features

Safer's primary features sit on the homepage of the Watch and can be accessed both manually as well as become active via the beacon based on the location of the nurse in the ER.

Schedule Check

ER nurses can check their shift schedule as soon as they enter the ER and the secure app becomes activated.

Patient List

Here, the ER nurses can view the patients assigned to them. This list gets updated by the backend system as patients get admitted and leave.

Quick Call

When in need of assistance, nurses can access a list of available staff to call or message.

Self-Monitoring

The nurse can quickly access an overview of her health stats without having to leave the app.

Voice-Activated Charting

Quick charting via the Apple Watch app converts speech to text and directly inputs information to the workstation for final approval.

Quick Call for Assistance

In case of need for assistance, the Apple Watch app shows a list of available staff, and notifies the selected person.

Stress Assessment

On detection of high respiratory and breathing rate over a considerable period, the Watch notifies the nurses to take a break.

Storyboard

The storyboard helped us to visualize how the Safer ecosystem would be used by the ER nurse at the hospital in various points of their jouney. It gave us clear idea about next step in journey of the design.

Web Console

All patient data recorded via the Apple Watch passes through the Patient Charting System on the desktop for a final verification to ensure error-free reporting. The medication recorded for patients get synced with the Medical Retrieval System on the iPad, making it easier to verify and collect the medication.
